Sleep Habits

Tweet
Sat 14 September 2013
By William Cox

In misc.

I've been tracking my sleep with a Jawbone Up. It logs "light", "deep" and awak periods during the night. I trigger it when I got to sleep and when I get back up, so I have a record of "time in bed" vs. when I fall asleep (roughly, it can be off sometimes). I've also recently set up an IFTTT trigger to send me a text message each morning to ask how my sleep was. This is very qualtative, but I figured worth tracking. I'm hoping that the sleep data paired with sleep quality will be interesting. I'm wondering if the sleep will impact future sleep. Ideally I want to have a definite way of tracking moood but nothing I'v tried so far works very well.

Here we see how much deep sleep (normalized by total time in bed) vs. awake time. Looks like the amount of time I spend awake doesn't impact how much deep sleep I get.

Obviously here deep and light sleep are inversely proportional (since it's normalized by time-in-bed).

So the question is a) does deep sleep matter and b) what impacts deep sleep?
