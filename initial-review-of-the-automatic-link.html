Initial Review of the Automatic Link

Tweet
Sat 07 September 2013
By William Cox

In misc.

I purchased a Link to measure my driving habits. One of the things I was most curious about was the cost of my commute. I have two some equal commute options and I wanted to know if there was a significant savings to one or the other. I only have 14 data points so far (as I split my commute twice a week for a workout) but here's the data so far:

Route 1 (17.78 miles) Cost - \$2.86/one way Route 2 (18.55 miles) Cost - \$2.75/one way

Weekly Cost Difference: \$1.0833

Route 1 Time - 27.25 minutes Route 2 Time - 28.33 minutes

Weekly Time Difference: 10.83 minutes

Now, this isn't the full story, since Route 1 is somewhat asymetric. It takes ~25.75 minutes to go and 30.25 minutes to come on Route 1, whereas Route 2 is pretty symetric. I should also note that I have 12 datapoints for Route 1 and only 6 for Route 2. Definitely not enough data.
